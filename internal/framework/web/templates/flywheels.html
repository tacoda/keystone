flywheels

Learning grows the harness; Pruning keeps it bounded. Both are agent-driven — they need an agent in the loop to do the reasoning. Each card shows the canonical slash command; click to copy, then paste into your host agent (Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, …) to fire the skill.

learn — additive

Captures a learning candidate into .keystone/harness/learning/inbox/ from a surprise, incident, or review finding. Subsequent /keystone:synthesize runs promote accepted candidates to guides/ or corpus/.

/keystone:learn

After firing, review the inbox at /flywheels/inbox and mark candidates accepted / rejected.

synthesize — promote

Walks learning/inbox/, promotes status: accepted candidates to the right layer (guide / corpus / sensor), then regenerates INDEX.json.

/keystone:synthesize

Inspect promotions afterward via /harness/primitives.

audit — subtractive

Periodic dual flywheel: walks the corpus + guides looking for stale or unused content; proposes archive moves with a one-line reason. The Pruning loop is asymmetric — guides churn often; corpus rarely.

/keystone:audit

See /flywheels/prune for the deterministic side (lint + heuristic) of the same loop.
